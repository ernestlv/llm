So "Euclidean geometry" specifically means

Space is flat — no curvature
Parallel lines never meet
Triangles always sum to 180°
Distance is always computed with the Pythagorean formula

These rules all come as a package. If you change one, you're in a different geometry with a different name.

Why it matters in practice
In machine learning for example, choosing a geometry is a real decision. If you measure distance between data points using the Pythagorean formula you are assuming the space is flat. But sometimes data lives on a curved surface — a sphere, a manifold — and using Euclidean distance gives you wrong answers. Switching geometry changes what "close" and "far apart" mean entirely.
